ENTITY ID: L-02A (WEREWOLF)

CLASSIFICATION: LYCANTHROPIC AGENT (WEREWOLF)
Description / Profile

Subject is a human infected with a heavily mutated strain of canine pathogen, hypothesized to be an ancient, stable variant of the Rabies virus. The infection causes irreversible cellular restructuring during cyclical transformation, which occurs every four (4) days. Contrary to folklore, transformation is NOT moon-dependent.

Threat Assessment
Designation: LUPINE HOST Harm Rating: 8 (Physical - Severe) Vulnerability: Specialized Pathogen Dispersal, Physical Trauma (Decapitation, Massive Organ Failure) Reported Sector: Wilderness Borders / Isolated Properties
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • PATHOGENESIS: Infection is transmitted via direct fluid exchange (bites/scratches). Highly aggressive, but aggression levels vary post-transformation.
  • TRANSFORMATION (CYCLICAL): Occurs every 96 hours post-infection. Form varies from large humanoids to full-wolf forms. Subject retains little to no memory of the shift.
  • RESILIENCE: Standard firearms are largely ineffective. Silver-based weaponry is a traditional countermeasure but is currently unverified by R&D.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 7-B: DO NOT attempt verbal communication or emotional de-escalation; the subject is neurologically compromised.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Lethal force is authorized. Prioritize non-contact neutralization via high-velocity rounds or explosive trauma.
  • TREATMENT: No known cure for the irreversible cellular changes. Containment is the only option.

ENTITY ID: S-11B (VAMPIRE)

CLASSIFICATION: SANGUINE INFILTRATOR (VAMPIRE)
Description / Profile

Humanoid entity that is biologically alive but requires consistent ingestion of human blood for metabolic function. Cannot consume solid foods. Displays no aging or signs of common disease. Often mistaken for other non-pathological criminals, allowing for widespread population dispersal.

Threat Assessment
Designation: BLOOD HOST Harm Rating: 6 (Subversion/Physical) Vulnerability: Blood Deprivation (2 Weeks max), Garlic Fumes, Standard Trauma Reported Sector: Urban Centers / High Population Density
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • HAEMATOPHAGIA: Requires blood intake every 7-14 days. Deprivation leads to extreme instability and aggression after 7 days.
  • MENTAL DOMINION: Subject can assert subtle forms of mental influence on humans (hypnotic suggestion).
  • ENHANCED SENSES: Exceptional night vision (eyes glow in low light) and superior olfactory detection (can track blood scent over long distances).
  • INOCULATION: Can infect others via fluid exchange. A 'soft-cure' exists to prevent further infection, but the host changes are irreversible.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 1-A: Lethal force is authorized, but the subject can be killed by any means that would kill a normal human. Target central nervous system.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Garlic extract dispersed as a fine mist causes uncontrollable spasms and aggression, disrupting Mental Dominion.
  • R&D NOTE: Fangs are highly durable and continuously grow if not naturally worn down by host activities.

ENTITY ID: CH-41E (GORGON)

CLASSIFICATION: CHIMERIC OBSERVER (GORGON)
Description / Profile

Extremely rare biogenic entity. The 'Gorgon' phenotype results from a specific, complex genetic pairing, with purebloods being nearly nonexistent. Most encounters involve hybrids who resemble humans but retain the ocular and epidermal traits.

Threat Assessment
Designation: PETRIFYING HOST Harm Rating: 9 (Irreversible Biological Damage - Direct) Vulnerability: Ocular Occlusion (Blindfold), Light Deprivation, Loud, Distracting Noise) Reported Sector: Highly Isolated Communities / Deep Forests
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • GAZE TRIGGER: The primary threat. Direct, sustained eye contact triggers an immediate, rapid onset of cellular mineralization, turning living tissue to stone.
  • OCULAR CONTROL: If trained from youth, the subject can learn to control the output of the gaze, allowing for normal interaction. Untrained or stressed subjects pose an extreme risk.
  • HYBRID TRAITS: Hybrids may only petrify certain materials or may have a delayed onset, making them harder to detect but slightly less lethal.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 9-G: ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY: Ocular occlusion (thick, padded blindfold) for containment or transport. Even trained subjects are unstable under duress.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Use reflective surfaces (mirrors) to monitor location without direct sight. Engage only with full-face shields.
  • OBSERVATION NOTES: The petrifying mechanism is hypothesized to be a highly complex, genetically driven biological defense mechanism, not a mystical effect.

ENTITY ID: ABC-01 (DRAGON)

CLASSIFICATION: APEX BIOTHERMAL CONSTRUCT (HIGH DANGER)
Description / Profile

A massive, genetically unique reptile, theorized to be either the last of an extinct order or a result of illegal R&D. It possesses specialized, paired pyrophoric glands that synthesize and store highly unstable, auto-igniting chemicals (similar to phosphine). The 'fire' is the result of forcefully ejecting and mixing these compounds upon contact with air.

Threat Assessment
Designation: FIRE-BREATHER Harm Rating: 10 (Environmental/Mass Destruction) Vulnerability: Cryogenic Agents, Internal Pressure Disruption, Water Immersion (Extremely High Volume) Reported Sector: Remote Caverns / Active Geothermal Zones
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • THERMAL INJECTION: Capable of incinerating targets from 50m. Thermal plume reaches 1,500°C. Requires time to replenish chemical stores after a prolonged attack.
  • ARMORED HIDE: Epidermal plates are reinforced with high-density keratin, making them highly resistant to standard high-explosive rounds.
  • FLIGHT: Possesses large, complex musculature for atmospheric lift. Observed to be agile despite size.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 10-D: Only engage with anti-tank or specialized cryogenic weapons systems. Standard neutralization is not possible.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Target the vent-like structures behind the mandibles; these are critical for chemical pressure regulation.
  • R&D NOTE: The ABC's core weakness is its massive caloric and hydration needs; prolonged engagement should aim to exhaust its internal reserves.

ENTITY ID: HGS-01 (GIANT)

CLASSIFICATION: HYPER-GLANDULAR SUBJECT (Troll/Ogre)
Description / Profile

A humanoid entity suffering from a rapid, aggressive pituitary disorder causing uncontrollable accelerated growth and extreme bone/muscle density (giantism). The entity possesses an overactive adrenal system, which leads to unpredictable, manic aggression and a rapid metabolic rate, fueling its size and cellular repair.

Threat Assessment
Designation: MEGA-MUSCLE Harm Rating: 8 (Physical - Crushing/Blunt Force) Vulnerability: High-Dose Sedatives, Extreme Caloric Deprivation, Sustained Internal Organ Trauma) Reported Sector: Industrial Wastelands / Mountain Passes
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • ACCELERATED REPAIR: Rapid metabolism grants a high-speed healing factor (the 'regeneration'), which is immediately degraded by lack of caloric intake.
  • BLUNT FORCE TRAUMA: Bone and muscle density make standard rounds ineffective. Attacks rely on crushing force.
  • AGGRESSION: Low intellectual function and high adrenal output make negotiation impossible.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 5-H: Requires specialized large-caliber tranquilizers (HGS-500 cocktail) targeted at the cerebral cortex.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Neutralize by targeting major arteries or inducing extreme blood loss, overwhelming the repair mechanism.
  • R&D NOTE: The subject must consume thousands of calories per day to maintain its repair rate; denial of food is a long-term neutralization strategy.

ENTITY ID: ISE-02 (MOTHMAN)

CLASSIFICATION: INFRASONIC EMITTER (Cryptid/Biological)
Description / Profile

A rare, large primate or humanoid carrier of a symbiotic insectoid organism, possibly airborne. The 'glowing eyes' are a result of bio-luminescence used to confuse prey/mates. The subject's massive wings generate a specific, patterned infrasound frequency that causes intense neurological effects in humans.

Threat Assessment
Designation: PSYCHO-ACOUSTIC HAZARD Harm Rating: 7 (Psychic/Neurological) Vulnerability: High-Density Ear Protection, Electromagnetic Noise, Strobe Lights Reported Sector: Overpasses / Large, Steel Structures / Power Plants
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • NEURO-ACOUSTIC DISPERSAL: Uses infrasound to induce symptoms in targets including vertigo, extreme anxiety, panic attacks, and "prophetic" visual/auditory distortions (the "omen").
  • THERMAL INVISIBILITY: High-speed flight creates a thermal wake, making it difficult to lock on with standard thermal imaging equipment.
  • PREDATORY LUMINANCE: Bio-luminescent eyes serve to draw and disorient prey.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 4-I: Handlers must wear specialized Acoustic Dampening Headgear at all times in active zones.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Target the wings or joints to disable flight and acoustic generation. Standard firearms are effective against the biological host once grounded.
  • R&D NOTE: The observed 'omens' are entirely a neurological artifact caused by the infrasound and should be disregarded by field teams.

ENTITY ID: BGF-07 (GRIFFIN)

CLASSIFICATION: BIOGENETIC FLIER (Avian/Feline Hybrid)
Description / Profile

A genetically engineered predator combining traits of a large raptor (eagle/condor) and a powerful terrestrial feline. The resulting creature has a massive wingspan (approx. 10m) and disproportionately large, powerful talons in its forelimbs. It is theorized to be a failed military bio-weapon due to its uncontrolled aggression and high metabolic consumption.

  • SUPERIOR SIGHT: Ocular structure grants extreme magnification and clarity, allowing target identification from over 5km away.

Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 3-G: Avoid aerial engagement. If flight is necessary, use decoy aircraft. Ground teams should utilize heavy cover.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Deploy concentrated electro-shock weapons to temporarily paralyze the subject and force it to the ground. Target the large, vulnerable flight muscles.
  • R&D NOTE: Its territorial defense strategy is always based on high-altitude advantage. Utilizing low-altitude, confined terrain negates its primary strength.

ENTITY ID: COT-08 (TATZELWURM)

CLASSIFICATION: CRYPTO-OPHIDIAN TOXIN (Mountain Serpent)
Description / Profile

A rare, massive limbless reptile (serpentine/amphisbaenian) found primarily in the Alpine regions. It is an ectothermic ambush predator with a unique bifurcated neurotoxin delivered via twin fangs. The 'cat head' is a misnomer; its broad skull is an evolutionary adaptation for gripping and crushing, with calcified frontal plates.

Threat Assessment
Designation: COLD VENOM Harm Rating: 8 (Toxicity/Ambush) Vulnerability: High-Frequency Noise, Thermal Overload (Fire/Heat), Anti-Toxin (Specialized) Reported Sector: High Altitude Caves / Glacier Edges / Hidden Valleys
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • NEUROTOXIC VENOM: One component causes rapid paralysis; the second component causes catastrophic central nervous system failure. Death occurs within 10 minutes.
  • THERMAL BLINDNESS: Extremely cold environment means standard thermal detection is often useless; relies on seismic and olfactory senses.
  • BURROWING: Capable of tunneling rapidly through packed snow and soft earth, allowing for silent, unexpected vertical attacks.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 8-C: Never engage alone. Use high-intensity sound generators (8-12 kHz range) to disorient and force it to expose itself.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Maintain a warm perimeter (flamethrowers, thermal blankets) to keep the subject sluggish. Only specialized, fast-acting anti-venom (COT-A) is effective.
  • OBSERVATION NOTE: It can survive for months in hibernation without food in sub-zero temperatures. Assume any deep cave is a potential nest.

The infection also induces territorial mania and hyper-aggression, transforming a benign herbivore into a vicious, bio-hazard threat.

Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • PATHOGEN TRANSMISSION: The primary threat is the spread of the virus via bodily fluids or aerosolized tumor fragments. Infection is rapid among other small mammals.
  • HYPER-AGGRESSION: The subject displays unnatural ferocity, charging targets regardless of size.

  • PROTOCOL 2-L: Neutralization is easy, but CONTAINMENT IS CRITICAL. Any area where a LPC-09 is neutralized must be immediately incinerated and decontaminated.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Use low-power incendiary rounds or controlled burn protocols to neutralize the host and sanitize the immediate environment.
  • R&D NOTE: The aggressive behavior is a side effect of the virus targeting the limbic system, not a conscious predatory action.

ENTITY ID: NCE-12A (UNICORN)

CLASSIFICATION: NEURO-CONDUCTIVE EQUINE (Biological EMP)
Description / Profile

A genetically 'pure' equine exhibiting an anomalous cranial structure: a single, dense, helical keratinous horn composed primarily of silica and crystalline calcium phosphate. This horn acts as a passive energy collector and amplifier, producing a highly focused, non-lethal bio-electric pulse capable of neurological interference.

Threat Assessment
Designation: NEURO-DISRUPTOR Harm Rating: 4 (Neurological/Crowd Control) Vulnerability: Horn Trauma, Electrical Insulation, Sonic Disruption (Interferes with charge cycle) Reported Sector: Protected Reserves / Untouched Wildlands
Recorded Movements (Abilities)
  • NEURO-STUN PULSE: Discharges a focused pulse, causing temporary paralysis, disorientation, and short-term memory loss (5m radius). The effect is often described by victims as a feeling of "purity" or "awe."
  • ENHANCED VITALITY: Exceptional longevity, near-total resistance to common illness, and rapid bone repair (biological strength).
  • HORN SCAN: The horn emits a low-power, non-visible electromagnetic field that allows the subject to 'map' its environment, including hidden or camouflaged threats.
Protocol & Countermeasures
  • PROTOCOL 12-U: Non-lethal capture is preferred. Subject is classified as highly valuable and should not be harmed. Handlers must use non-conductive restraints.
  • COUNTERMEASURE: Employ specialized electrical insulation armor (Faraday mesh) to shield personnel and equipment during capture attempts. Sonic interference can disrupt its charging process.
  • OBSERVATION NOTE: Highly sensitive to negative emotions and distress; will only approach individuals deemed "calm" or "neutral."
